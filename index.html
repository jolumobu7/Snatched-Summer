Why Cardio + Diet Is the Real Transformation

The lifts build your shape — bigger glutes, tighter waist. But if body fat is covering those muscles, you won't see them. Cardio and a proper diet lower the body fat that hides the work you're putting in. You need both.

⚡ The formula: Lifting builds and emphasizes your physique. Cardio + diet reveals it. One without the other is half the job.

🏃‍♀️ Stairmaster

20–30 min every single day. Low-intensity walking burns fat without stressing recovery. The most underrated waist tool there is.

Diet 101
The Only Things That Actually Matter
⚖️

Calorie Deficit = Fat Loss

To lose body fat you need to eat fewer calories than you burn. No magic foods, no detox teas. Consistent deficit over time is the only thing that lowers body fat. Aim for 300–500 calories under maintenance.

🥩

Protein Protects Your Muscle

When you diet, your body can break down muscle for energy if you don't eat enough protein. Eating ~0.7–1g per lb of lean mass keeps your glutes while fat comes off. Prioritize protein every meal.

🍞

Carbs Aren't the Enemy

Carbs fuel your workouts. Without them, lifts suffer and energy crashes. Eat quality carbs — rice, oats, sweet potato, fruit — around your workouts. Don't eliminate them.

🛑

What Actually Kills Progress

Liquid calories (drinks, alcohol, juices), ultra-processed snacks, skipping meals then binging, and underestimating portions. Fix these four things first — they account for most failed diets.

💧 Hydration Is Non-Negotiable

Dehydration causes bloating, makes body fat look worse, kills your energy in workouts, and slows fat loss. Proper hydration is one of the fastest ways to look leaner even before you lose fat. Goal: half your bodyweight in ounces daily, more on training days.

🌅
Morning Habit

16–24oz of water before your first meal. Every single day, no exceptions.

🏃
Around Training

16oz before, sip during, 16–24oz after every session to replace what you sweat out.

🍋
Make It Easy

Add lemon, cucumber, or mint if plain water is hard. Sugar-free electrolyte packets help too.
